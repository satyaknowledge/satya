Dalai Lama - Ocean of Wisdom

Narender Singh February 27, 2020
The Dalai Lama is the 14th Dharma Guru of the ancient tradition of Tibet. The Dalai Lama is a title, meaning the ocean of wisdom. For the first time, this title was given to Sonam Gyats in the late 16th century by King Altan Khan Aim of Mongolia in his honor.

Who knew that this child of a poor peasant family of the Adi province of eastern Tibet would be worshiped as a 'living Buddha' by the Buddhist religions of the world.

The original name of the Dalai Lama is Tenzin Gyatso. At the age of two, Tibetans began to consider him as an incarnation of the previous Dalai Lama. Even today, 60 million Tibetans consider him the avatar of Avalokiteshvara (chain regi), the god of mercy, but the very humble and self-conscious Dalai Lama considers himself nothing more than a Buddhist monk.

When the army of Mao-tse-Tung invaded Tibet in 1950, at the age of 15, the Dalai Lama had to go to Beijing and negotiate a settlement with Mao. The freedom movement of Tibet against Chinese imperialism had failed in 1959, when the Dalai Lama was forced to take refuge in India. Since then, he is living in an area called 'Dharamshala' in Himachal Pradesh with one lakh Tibetan refugees.

Dharamshala as 'Nanhe Lhasa' (Lhasa is the capital of Tibet) has become the headquarters of the Dalai Lama's exiled government today. Mahatma Gandhi is the inspiration of the Dalai Lama. He has unwavering faith in peace and non-violence.

The Dalai Lama says- "I have full faith in Mahatma Gandhi's path of non-violence. I have always been inspired by this and I hope that the question of Tibet will be resolved. There is no other way than non-violence."

There is no doubt that after receiving the Nobel Peace Prize, the Dalai Lama's international image as a peaceful and non-violent leader has been created. The Dalai Lama himself accepts this fact and says, as a monk, I should not be influenced by the awards.
