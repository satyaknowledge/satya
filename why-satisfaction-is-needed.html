Why Satisfaction is needed?

Narender Singh March 8, 2020

Man's life is a reflection of his thoughts. Success-failure, progress-decadence, insignificance-greatness, happiness-sorrow, peace, etc. all depend on the thoughts of human beings first.

Knowing the thoughts of any person, the map of his life can be easily found out. His thoughts have an important place in making a man anything cowardly, healthy-unhealthy, happy-unhappy. It means that the life of a human being is formed and worsens according to his thoughts. If good thoughts make it advanced, then inferior thoughts will fall down.

Swami Ramatirtha has said- "A man's life is like that."

Swami Vivekananda had said- "Heaven and hell are nowhere else, they live in our thoughts only."

Lord Buddha, while teaching his disciples, said- "Monks! Whatever we are in the present, our thoughts are due to us and whatever we become in the future is also due to our thoughts."

Shakespeare wrote - "Nothing is good or bad. The basis of good and evil is our thoughts."

Jesus said- "He becomes like a man has thoughts."

The famous Roman philosopher Marcus Aurelius said - "Whatever our life is, it is the result of our own thoughts."

The famous American writer Dale Carnegie, while revealing the facts based on his experiences, wrote - "The most important thing I have learned in life is that the power and importance of ideas, the power of ideas is supreme and immense."

In short, our thoughts have a major hand in conducting various activities of life. Whatever we do, we do it with the inspiration of ideas.
Variations and oddities seen in the world are also reflections of our thoughts.

The world is a shadow of human thoughts. For some, the world is heaven, for some, hell. For some, if the world is the face of peace, trouble, pain, etc., for someone, there is a garden full of happiness. Under the same circumstances, there is an extraordinary difference in the difference of opinion between two people with the same prosperity.

In one life, training experiences happiness, peace, satisfaction, and the other spends life in times of pain, grief. Not only this, many people are happy even after spending a life lacking difficulty, and many consider life to be hellish, even after being prosperous. One person remains contented in his circumstances and thanks God for life, the other remains dissatisfied even after getting many pleasures. Curses others, merely because of their own thoughts.

The ancient sage-sage Aranyak lived a happy and happy life after eating life of candle fruit and lived in a heavenly feeling on earth. Whereas today, there is a human who is troubled by more tribulation, disturbance, sorrow, ecstasy, even after living a life with ample comfort, prosperity, Aishwarya, scientific means. This is the result of thinking of humans.

Famous English writer Swart used to mourn each of his birthdays by wearing black and ragged clothes. He used to say- "I would not have got this life, I would not have come in the world."

In contrast, the blind poet Milton used to say- "Thank God, who gave me the priceless gift of living".

Napoleon Bonaparte said in his last days - "Sadly, I did not spend a week of my life in peace." While there was no shortage of prosperity, opulence, wealth, fame etc.

Alexander the Great also died in his last life, repenting. The basis of happiness, happiness or sorrow, grief, restlessness, remorse etc. in life are the thoughts of man himself. None other

Due to wrong thoughts, a person will be sad even in prosperity, opulence, rich life, and will experience happiness, peace and happiness even in a life which is lacking from excellent ideas.
