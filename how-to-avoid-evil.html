How to avoid evil? - Great Thoughts

Narender Singh February 28, 2020
The famous scholar Tacitus has said that "As long as humans live, evil will remain."

The greatest feature of evil is that it forces the holder to find his / her ambition elsewhere. As a result, no one is able to see their evils and does not even try to remove them. As a result, he continues to provide the birth protection of his evildoer and his unrestrained side continues to be confirmed.

Per-dosha philosophy means that a person who is busy in seeing the evil of others, remains ignorant of the insects inside him towards his evil. This is why it is said that Dosh Darshan is the biggest flaw or evil But the situation is such that no one blames himself in 99 percent of situations, no matter how big his mistake is.

Swami Ramathirtha says that 'अपनी आँख में शहतीर देख पाने की अपेक्षा दूसरों की आँख में तिनका देख लेना सरल है.'

Therefore, we should first avoid the evil called dosha-darshan and the simple solution to this is that we should practice watching our evils. We will see that the evils that we see in other people are present in us. Saint Kabir Das has said that

बुरा जो देखन मैं चला, बुरा मिला न कोय । जो दिल खोजा आपना, मुझसा बुरा न कोय ॥

Therefore, if we know our evils and try to remove them one by one, we will do a lot of work in life and ourselves will also become a good and big person. As soon as we start looking towards our faults or our evils, as we start to consider others as bad and blame them for our sufferings.

A famous thinker has spoken of great importance in this context - "Ordinary people blame each other for their evil."

Thinking of evil in any form casts a dark shadow on the mind. Clashes and discord arise due to the thought of evil or its discussion. The action of which karma results in discord and affliction, there is no benefit, but only loss. The best and best way to avoid evils is not to discuss them, and try to remove our evils one by one. Goods will be reputed by them in their spaces.

Acharya Vinoba Bhave has said that "Bad things should be forgotten. If we keep looking at the bad things, then people will become cruel."

Benjamin Franklin has also said- "Let your evils die before you."
