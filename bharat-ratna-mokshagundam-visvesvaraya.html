Bharat Ratna Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya - A great Inspiration

Narender Singh March 2, 2020

Bharat Ratna Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was born on 15 September 1861 in the village Madan Halli of Kolar district of Mysore state. His father Pandit Srinivas Shastri was a distinguished scholar, a religious but very poor and ordinary person.

He did not have the funds to teach his son. After studying in the village, he had to go to Bangalore for further education. He used to dine at his maternal uncle's house and pay tuition for a fee. The British Principal of the college, Charles Walters, impressed by his ability, talent and efficiency, got him enrolled in Science College of Poona and got scholarship.

In this college, he made full use of his time and got the first place in the Engineering Examination of Bombay University in 1883, as a result of which in 1884 he was awarded P.W. D. Appointed to the post of Assistant Engineer in the department. Due to his qualifications, he was about to take the post of Chief Engineer in a short time, but the British engineers opposed the posture of an Indian to the post of Chief Engineer. As a result, he was not made the Chief Engineer.

During the government service, he built the famous Sakkhar Dam in 1893 and invented the block system of irrigation and automatic sluice gates in 1899, which was praised by well-known politicians like Shri Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Mahadev Govind Ranade etc.

In 1908, he resigned from the government job. After resigning from a government job, he traveled abroad to see the industries there. In the same period, Nizam Hyderabad called itself to solve the problem of severe floods in his state. He solved the problem of flooding there by building a dam on Musi and its tributaries.

Chief Engineer

After this he was called by the Maharaja of Mysore and appointed as the Chief Engineer of the Mysore State. In 1912, the Krishnaraja Sagar Dam was built on the Kaveri River. Before the dam was built, the big British engineers had said that this dam cannot be built and crores of rupees will be wasted on it. With the construction of this dam, he got fame in the whole world.

In 1912, he was made the Diwan of the Kingdom of Mysore. He remained in the post of Diwan till 1918. During this period he started many industries and businesses there, among which Bhadravati's steel factory is notable. Established the University of Mysore in the field of education. It was the sixth university in India. People talk about their efficient and successful administration even today.

He was the first person to work on the planned economy in the country and on this subject he published his book Planned Economy for India. This was the first book of its subject published in India. He was later made the director of Tata Ispat, a post he held from 1927 to 1955.

At the age of about 90, he surprised the whole nation by constructing the Ganges bridge at Mokama near Patna. The entire country expressed respect for his merit, effort and amazing organization power. In this connection, Andhra Pradesh University in 1931, Kashi Hindu University in 1937, S.N. N. DT Women's University, Bombay and University of Mysore in 1948 awarded honorary titles.

Honors

The British Government of India honored him with the title of 'Sir'. On September 7, 1955, at the Rashtrapati Bhavan Delhi, the first President of independent India, Dr. Rajendra Prasad decorated him with the "Bharat Ratna". In 1958, the premier Royal Asiatic Society of Bengal honored him with the Durga Prasad Khaitan Gold Medal.

He died on April 14, 1962 after spending a full age of one hundred and one years with great respect and effort. Indian Engineers celebrate his birthday on 15 September every year as "Engineers Day" in the memory of this great Indian engineer.
