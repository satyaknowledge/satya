Should You go to England to Study?

Narender Singh February 28, 2020
It is natural for talented and talented students to have an ambition to get higher education in foreign universities and institutes. In the absence of sufficient information and proper guidelines, the idea of ​​getting education abroad from them seems like breaking stars from the sky, but all this is not true.

Talented and promising students who are not very rich and want to pursue higher education abroad, can know the way forward for their future by learning about the feelings of scholarship in foreign universities and institutes. Come, give you some useful information in this subject, so that you too can fulfill your dream of getting higher education abroad.

The education system of India is very much in line with the education system of England. Apart from this, a large number of people of Indian origin are inhabited there. That's why Indian people often like to go to England more for studies and it is often that after completing their education most of the people also work there.

The same happens to Indians known for research and higher education. Perhaps this is the reason that a large number of engineers, doctors and other trained human resources working in England belong to people of Indian origin. There are some who really want to return home, but do not have the courage to end the facilities available in England, the social environment and the relationships of their children's British life in one stroke. Hence, even if the unsolicited get away from their motherland.

There are many types of misconceptions in England about the system of foreign students, especially Indian students, to get education. Like some people say that to get education in England, English language proficiency is necessary. In the opinion of such people, English is the most difficult in the UK and the level of English language is also important along with education there.

There is no obstacle for students studying in convent or other public schools in India, but students studying through Hindi medium must face many difficulties there.

There are a total of 47 universities and institutes for higher education in England, many of which provide scholarships to foreign students by providing scholarships. Apart from this, there are also scholarships from Commonwealth countries and the British Council.

In universities here, applications for admission to postgraduate level courses are accepted through the Education Officer of the Indian Embassy in UK.
